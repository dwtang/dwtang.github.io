I am a postdoctoral researcher at Ming Hsieh Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering of University of Southern California. I work with Prof. Ashutosh Nayyar, Prof. Rahul Jain, and Prof. Pierluigi Nuzzo on control and learning for multi-agent systems. Prior to joining USC I was a postdoctoral researcher at University of California, Berkeley. I worked with Prof. Venkat Anantharam on understanding mathematical properties related to decomposition of correlated equilibrium.

My general research interest involves design and analysis of control and learning algorithms in stochastic dynamic systems, with a focus on systems that involve multiple agents with asymmetrical information. I strive to propose and understand conceptually simple solutions for those systems with tools from control theory, probability theory, and game theory. Some of my past work has been on stochastic dynamic games among teams, queuing systems, and LRU caching algorithm.

In my spare time I read non-academic books, listen to (non-academic?) music, play folk guitar, watch soccer, and bikes at the beachside bikeways in Santa Monica and Venice.
